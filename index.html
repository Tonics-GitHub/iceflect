"Icelandic inflectional patterns"




On this page you can define and view tables of inflectional patterns for Icelandic words. Start with clicking some of the buttons on the left to view precomputed tables. This should give you an idea about how to use the drop-down menus to define your own table structures. The word classes and inflectional categories used can be found here. A list of the possible inflections can be found here. Note that this page consistently uses the terms "indefinite" and "definite" to refer to the strong and weak forms of adjectives respectively. Dealing with verb conjugation, especially when the last three inflectional categories are involved, can currently be clunky and inflexible. This will hopefully be fixed in the future.
The affixes of a pattern consist of characters that differ between the relevant inflectional forms, i.e. those described in the tables. This means that the resulting affixes may change depending on how the tables are defined. The analysis is very imperfect and should be taken with a grain of salt. The patterns are sorted in order of frequency of which a word adhering to the pattern appears in Icelandic texts, out of all words in the word class. This is the percentage found under the tables. The words under that are the 20 most common words adhering to the pattern.

The inflection data is adapted from The Database of Icelandic Morphology (DIM), also known as Beygingarlýsing íslensks nútímamáls (BÍN), by the Árni Magnússon Institute for Icelandic Studies. The author and editor of DIM is Kristín Bjarnadóttir. CC BY-SA 4.0.
The data has been filtered, reformatted and augmented with frequency data. The head words and inflectional forms themselves remain unchanged.
The frequency data is adapted from Frequency lists for Icelandic by Þórunn Arnardóttir and Anton Karl Ingason. CC BY 4.0. The frequency list used is based on the Icelandic Gigaword Corpus (IGC-2022) by Starkaður Barkarson et al.
